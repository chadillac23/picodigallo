What's Happening
  • Choosing the technology platform for the app
  • Designing the plant database — the structure that holds every plant's details
  • Researching USDA Extension Office data for soil-testing connections
  • Establishing how you and I will work together throughout the project
What You Get

A confirmed technology direction, a data plan covering all plant information, and a working relationship for how you provide input throughout the build.

What It Enables

Everything downstream. We can't build the app until we know what platform it runs on and what data structure each plant uses.

What It Requires From You

Your input on which plant fields matter most, plant categories to include, and your data priorities.

Why This Order
The plant database is the heart of the app. If we built the app shell first without knowing what data each plant needs, we'd have to rebuild it when the data structure changes. Getting this right first means everything that follows fits together.
What's Happening
  • Building the actual app — landing page, and input screens for shade, soil, and zone
  • Building the plant recommendation engine
  • Building plant detail pages with all data fields (soil pH, water needs, sun hours, flowering periods, disease resistance, growth habit, toxicity warnings, maintenance)
  • Adding plant photos
What You Get

A working app on your phone with real plant data, searchable by your yard conditions. You input your shade, soil, and zone, and get plant recommendations with full details — soil pH, water needs, sun hours, flowering periods, disease resistance, growth habit, toxicity warnings, and maintenance requirements.

What It Enables

A functional app that can be tested and refined before adding the advanced features in Phase 3.

What It Requires
  • Phase 1 complete — platform chosen, database designed
  • Your review of plant data accuracy
  • Your feedback on the app's look and feel
Why This Order
The core app must work before we add smart features. The plant recommendation engine needs the database from Phase 1. The detail pages need all the data fields you specified. Building this first gives us a solid, testable product to refine before adding geolocation, photo upload, and AI features.
What's Happening
  • Geolocation — auto-detect your growing zone from your location
  • USDA Extension Office finder — locate the nearest office for soil testing
  • Photo upload of your yard with dimensions
  • Planting scheme suggestions — algorithmic layout based on your space
  • Vegetables section
  • Watering systems section
What You Get

The app detects your location and auto-fills your zone. You can photograph your planting area, input its dimensions, and get layout suggestions. The Extension Office finder connects you to soil-testing services. Vegetables and watering systems expand the app's usefulness.

What It Enables

The data foundation for AI-powered features in Phase 4's future vision. Photo upload and dimensions are prerequisites for AI growth-progression images.

What It Requires
  • Phase 2 complete — working app with plant database and recommendation engine
  • Geolocation needs the zone database (from Phase 1)
  • Planting schemes need the plant data plus your dimensions
Why This Order
Smart features depend on the core app. You can't suggest a planting scheme without the plant database and conditions engine from Phase 2. You can't auto-detect zones without the zone lookup data from Phase 1. And the photo upload in Phase 3 is itself a prerequisite for the AI features later — the AI needs to know what your yard looks like before it can generate growth images.
What's Happening
  • Publishing to the Apple App Store
  • Publishing to the Google Play Store
  • Publishing to the web
  • Writing user documentation
  • Creating a collaboration runbook
  • Setting up the deployment pipeline
What You Get

A published app that anyone can download or visit online. Professional documentation. An ongoing maintenance process.

What It Enables

Public availability, user acquisition, and a potential monetization path.

What It Requires
  • Phase 2 complete — a minimum viable product
  • Phase 3 features are optional for initial launch, but recommended
Why This Order
You need a working app before you can publish it. App Store and Play Store review processes take time, so we submit only when the app is polished and stable — not before.
Future Vision — not yet scheduled

These features are not yet scheduled. The current roadmap is designed so the app can accommodate all of them later without a rebuild. Tap any item to see what it depends on.
